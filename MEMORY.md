# Memory: Callum Whitridge

## Personal Profile

Callum James Whitridge is a Mountain West kid who became a Pacific Northwest adult, and that seam runs through most of how he sees himself. He grew up in Boise, raised by a father who fixed things with his hands and a household where work done well was the actual marker of self-respect. He earned a B.A. in History at Ridgeline University, Boise, 2017, and an M.Ed. in Curriculum and Instruction at Cascade Pacific University, Eugene, 2021 (part-time, while teaching). Two years of college French, almost none retained.

He reads as steady, the calm trained rather than innate. Off the clock the impatience leaks: fingers drumming at a red light, podcasts abandoned the moment the host wanders, a flat exhale when the grocery line crawls. He is slow to name what bothers him, will carry a low irritation for a week, and then resolve it in one uncharacteristically blunt sentence that surprises whoever is on the receiving end.

Underneath the calm is somebody who wants badly to be good at things and is mildly embarrassed by how much that matters to him. He keeps score in games he claims not to care about and measures himself against a standard he would never impose on anyone else. Kindest to others on the terms he refuses to extend to himself.

Single. He dated someone for about a year, ending in fall 2025 when she moved to Seattle for a job. Not actively on apps, says he is "taking a break," would admit that mostly means he has not gotten around to it.

## Work & Projects

Callum teaches 7th-grade U.S. History at Cedar Ridge Middle School, a public school of roughly 650 students in Eugene. After his B.A. (Ridgeline University, Boise, 2017) he student-taught at Fern Valley Middle School in Boise in spring 2018, then moved from Boise to Eugene in 2019, substitute-taught across Lane County schools while completing M.Ed. coursework at Cascade Pacific University, and joined Cedar Ridge full-time in fall 2020. He is now in his sixth year on staff. He holds an active Oregon teaching license through TSPC (Teacher Standards and Practices Commission), issued 2020, multiple-subject middle-grades endorsement. He runs five periods a day, with prep period 3 from 10:45 to 11:30 AM. He stays until about 3:30 or 4:00 PM most days for grading and prep, then runs cross-country practice from 3:15 to 4:45 PM during the August through November season.

His current academic focus is the founding-era and early-republic unit. He is also writing first-semester assessments, prepping the 7th-grade field trip to the Oregon Historical Society in Portland, and coaching through the championship meets.

He reports to Laura Chen (department chair, direct) and Dave Harmon (principal). His school email at the district address is `cwhitridge@cedarridgemiddle.edu`. Grading runs through PowerGrade on the school-issued HP Chromebook. He has 12 sick days a year plus the standard school breaks. Laura Chen is the designated operational-escalation contact for school matters.

His step on the district scale is 6 with an M.Ed. bump. Standing professional commitments include the Cedar Ridge social-studies department blog (read-only contributions through Laura), the cross-country team page hosted on Webflow, and occasional PD invitations from Cascade Pacific University.

## Health & Wellness

Callum is generally healthy. Active lifestyle. No chronic conditions, no known allergies, no daily medications or supplements. He takes ibuprofen occasionally after long runs.

His care team is in Eugene. Primary care is Dr. Angela Torres at Willamette Family Health Clinic, with annual physicals. Dental is Dr. Howard Park at Emerald Valley Dental, every six months. Vision is Dr. Yuki Tanabe at Eugene Vision Center, annually. He wears glasses for mild astigmatism, diagnosed at age 16.

He runs three to four times a week, three to five miles each, mixing the campus trail and neighborhood loops, with longer trail runs on weekends at Ridgeline Trail and Spencer Butte. He runs with the team some days during cross-country season. Occasional pickup basketball at the YMCA on Wednesday evenings. He has no diagnosed mental-health conditions. He gets restless during the long gray Pacific Northwest winter stretch (he calls it "Oregon February brain") and manages it with exercise and staying busy.

## Interests & Hobbies

Woodworking is the anchor and counterweight to a day spent talking. Hand tools over power tools, partly on principle, partly because slowness is the point. Aspiration: real furniture and proud joinery. Actual output: boxes, a turned bowl, a stubborn run of cutting boards. Saturday mornings at Timber and Tenon with Rick Sutherland.

Running is the release valve. Logs every run on Strava through the Garmin Forerunner 245. Circling a fall 2026 half-marathon, leaning toward the Eugene-area race over Portland Marathon Half or Bend Half Marathon, and "going to commit before registration fills."

He reads history and narrative nonfiction in paper, currently "The Coalfield Dispatches" by Henry Kline (labor history), bought from Tsunami Books. Follows Oregon Ducks football and basketball, runs a $50 buy-in 12-team fantasy football league with Sam and college friends, NFL Sundays in fall and winter. Comfort sitcom rewatches (Parks and Recreation, again) run in the background while attention drifts to grading or planning.

Travel is by car. Oregon coast worked through. Montana and Wyoming loop kept in back pocket. Camping or cheap motels. Not built for sitting still on a beach.
